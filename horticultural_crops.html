Horticultural Crops in Maharashtra

Horticultural crops refer to fruits, vegetables, flowers, and other high-value crops cultivated for food, ornamental purposes, and medicinal or aromatic use. Maharashtra, due to its diverse agro-climatic zones, is one of the leading states in India for horticulture. The state's favorable climate, irrigation facilities, and technological advancements have made it a hub for various horticultural crops.

Types of Horticultural Crops in Maharashtra:



1. Fruits in Maharashtra

Maharashtra is a major producer of fruits, especially tropical and subtropical varieties. The state produces a significant share of India’s national fruit output. The following are some key fruit crops:

a. Mango (Mangifera indica)

  • Regions: Konkan region, including Ratnagiri, Sindhudurg, and parts of Western Maharashtra.
  • Climate: Requires a warm and humid climate, with well-distributed rainfall (75-250 cm). The temperature range should be 24-30°C.
  • Soil: Prefers well-drained lateritic or loamy soil, rich in organic matter.
  • Varieties: Alphonso (Hapus), Kesar, Pairi.
  • Harvesting Season: March to June.
  • Challenges: Prone to pests like fruit flies and diseases like anthracnose.

b. Banana (Musa spp.)

  • Regions: Grown in Jalgaon, Dhule, Nanded, and parts of Western Maharashtra.
  • Climate: Requires a tropical climate with temperatures between 25-35°C and annual rainfall of 100-250 cm.
  • Soil: Thrives in deep, well-drained, fertile soils with a high moisture retention capacity.
  • Varieties: Dwarf Cavendish, Basrai, Grand Naine.
  • Harvesting Season: Throughout the year, but the main season is September to April.
  • Challenges: Susceptible to pests like weevils and diseases like Panama wilt and Sigatoka.

c. Grapes (Vitis vinifera)

  • Regions: Grown mainly in Nashik, Sangli, Solapur, and Pune.
  • Climate: Requires a warm and dry climate for growth, with temperatures between 15-35°C.
  • Soil: Best suited for loamy, well-drained soils with a neutral to slightly alkaline pH.
  • Varieties: Thompson Seedless, Sonaka, Sharad Seedless, and Tas-A-Ganesh.
  • Harvesting Season: February to April.
  • Uses: Mainly used for fresh consumption and for wine-making.
  • Challenges: Vulnerable to downy mildew, powdery mildew, and pests like mealybugs.

d. Pomegranate (Punica granatum)

  • Regions: Grown in Solapur, Sangli, Ahmednagar, Nashik, and Pune.
  • Climate: Requires a hot, dry climate with temperatures between 25-35°C.
  • Soil: Thrives in well-drained loamy or black soils with good organic content.
  • Varieties: Bhagwa, Ganesh, Arakta.
  • Harvesting Season: June to September and December to February.
  • Challenges: Prone to bacterial blight, fruit rot, and aphid attacks.

e. Citrus (Lemon, Sweet Lime, and Oranges)

  • Regions: Grown in Nagpur, Wardha, Amravati, and Vidarbha region.
  • Climate: Requires a semi-arid climate with moderate rainfall (75-150 cm) and temperatures between 20-30°C.
  • Soil: Well-drained sandy loam or loamy soils with good organic content.
  • Varieties: Nagpur Orange, Sathgudi Sweet Lime, Kagzi Lime.
  • Harvesting Season: November to January (Oranges), Throughout the year (Lime).
  • Challenges: Prone to citrus canker, gummosis, and pests like citrus psylla.

2. Vegetables in Maharashtra

Maharashtra is a significant producer of vegetables, with farmers cultivating both short-duration and long-duration crops.

a. Tomato (Solanum lycopersicum)

  • Regions: Grown in Pune, Nashik, Ahmednagar, and Satara.
  • Climate: Prefers warm weather with temperatures between 20-30°C. Extreme heat and cold affect yield.
  • Soil: Grows well in well-drained loamy soils rich in organic content.
  • Varieties: Pusa Ruby, Arka Meghali, and Vaishali.
  • Sowing Season: October to February.
  • Challenges: Susceptible to pests like fruit borers and diseases like bacterial wilt and blight.

b. Brinjal (Eggplant) (Solanum melongena)

  • Regions: Grown in Pune, Nashik, and Vidarbha region.
  • Climate: Requires a warm climate with temperatures between 25-30°C.
  • Soil: Thrives in well-drained, fertile loamy soils.
  • Varieties: Arka Nidhi, Pusa Purple Long, and Bhagyamati.
  • Sowing Season: Throughout the year.
  • Challenges: Prone to pests like fruit borers and aphids, and diseases like bacterial wilt.

c. Onion (Allium cepa)

  • Regions: Major crop in Nashik, Pune, Ahmednagar, and Solapur.
  • Climate: Grows best in mild climates with temperatures ranging from 13-25°C.
  • Soil: Requires well-drained sandy loam soils rich in organic matter.
  • Varieties: N-53, Agrifound Light Red, and Pusa Red.
  • Sowing Season: June to July (Kharif), October to December (Rabi).
  • Challenges: Prone to diseases like purple blotch, stemphyllium blight, and pests like thrips.

d. Cabbage (Brassica oleracea var. capitata)

  • Regions: Grown in Pune, Satara, and parts of Vidarbha.
  • Climate: Prefers a cool climate with temperatures between 15-20°C.
  • Soil: Thrives in well-drained loamy soils rich in organic content.
  • Varieties: Golden Acre, Pusa Drumhead.
  • Sowing Season: July to October.
  • Challenges: Susceptible to pests like diamondback moth and diseases like black rot.

3. Flowers in Maharashtra

The floriculture sector in Maharashtra has been expanding, driven by both domestic demand and export potential. Some major flowers grown are:

a. Rose (Rosa spp.)

  • Regions: Grown in Pune, Nashik, and Satara.
  • Climate: Requires a mild climate with temperatures between 15-25°C.
  • Soil: Grows best in well-drained loamy soils with good organic content.
  • Varieties: Hybrid Tea, Floribunda, and Damask roses.
  • Uses: For ornamental purposes, perfumes, and medicinal uses.
  • Challenges: Prone to diseases like powdery mildew and pests like thrips and aphids.

b. Marigold (Tagetes spp.)

  • Regions: Grown in Pune, Nashik, and Solapur.
  • Climate: Requires a warm climate with temperatures between 20-30°C.
  • Soil: Thrives in well-drained sandy loam soils.
  • Varieties: African Marigold, French Marigold.
  • Uses: Widely used in religious ceremonies and ornamental purposes.
  • Challenges: Susceptible to pests like aphids and diseases like leaf spot.

4. Spices and Condiments in Maharashtra

a. Turmeric (Curcuma longa)

  • Regions: Grown in Satara, Sangli, and parts of Western Maharashtra.
  • Climate: Requires a hot and humid climate with temperatures between 20-30°C and well-distributed rainfall.
  • Soil: Grows best in well-drained loamy soils rich in organic content.
  • Varieties: Salem, Rajapuri, Prabha.
  • Uses: Primarily used as a spice, in cosmetics, and medicinal preparations.
  • Challenges: Prone to diseases like rhizome rot and pests like shoot borers.

b. Garlic (Allium sativum)

  • Regions: Grown in Nashik, Pune, and Ahmednagar.
  • Climate: Requires a mild climate with temperatures between 12-25°C.
  • Soil: Prefers well-drained loamy soils rich in organic content.
  • Varieties: Yamuna Safed, Agrifound Parvati.
  • Uses: Extensively used in culinary dishes and for medicinal purposes.
  • Challenges: Prone to fungal diseases like rust and pests like thrips.

5. Medicinal and Aromatic Plants

a. Aloe Vera (Aloe barbadensis)

  • Regions: Grown in Vidarbha and Marathwada.
  • Climate: Requires a warm climate with temperatures between 20-30°C and moderate rainfall.
  • Soil: Thrives in well-drained sandy or loamy soils.
  • Uses: Known for its medicinal properties and used in cosmetics.
  • Challenges: Susceptible to pests like mealybugs and diseases like leaf spot.
